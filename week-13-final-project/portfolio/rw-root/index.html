On this day in history...

October 27th

Below is a collection of 8 events that occured on the day of October 27th. There are many important, serious, and fun events that occured on this day in history.

Quakers executed for religious beliefs - William Robinson and Marmaduke Stevenson, two Quakers who came from England in 1656 to escape religious persecution, are executed in the Massachusetts Bay Colony for their religious beliefs. The two had violated a law passed by the Massachusetts General Court the year before, banning Quakers from the colony under penalty of death.

Philadelphia was founded by English entrepreneur William Penn. Penn received the land as a payment to fulfill a debt that King Charles II owed to Penn’s father. Philadelphia is the only UNESCO-declared World Heritage City in the United States and it was temporarily the capital of the United States in the 1800s.

Theodore Roosevelt is born - future President Theodore Roosevelt is born in New York City to the wealthy Roosevelt family. Roosevelt was home-schooled and then attended Harvard University, graduating in 1880. In September 14th, 1901, he seceded deceased President McKinley as the next president of the United States.

Mayor George McClellan takes the controls on the inaugural run of New York City’s innovative new rapid transit system- the subway



Du Pont announced "nylon" as the new name for its new synthetic yarn.

ABC premiered Walt Disney’s first TV show, “Disneyland.”

NASA’s Saturn I rocket was tested for the first time in Mission Saturn-Apollo 1.

Complicated and tension-filled negotiations between the United States and the Soviet Union finally result in a plan to end the two-week-old Cuban Missile Crisis A frightening period in which nuclear holocaust seemed imminent began to come to an end.
